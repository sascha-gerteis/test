Performance Evaluation

What worked: Very cheap reach and impressions (strong media efficiency).
What did not work: 2,000+ messages, 0 qualified leads, 0 measurable revenue impact.
Interpretation:
  • Delivery appears optimized for the cheapest audience.
  • Low-cost audiences tend to send “Hi / default message” with no follow-up.
  • This behavior correlates with low purchase intent (tickets / travel).

Key Insight

Recommendation (Next Step)

Strategy shift: Optimize for qualified leads (buyers), not the cheapest CPM.
  • Stop broad low-intent targeting within India (cheap CPM is often driven by Tier 2/3 cities and low purchasing power segments).
  • Refine India targeting toward higher-value audiences by focusing on Tier 1 cities (Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Ahmedabad) and layering high-income travel and luxury behavior signals.
  • Target users showing strong Thailand travel intent (Bangkok, Phuket, Pattaya, Thailand Tourism) and frequent international travel behavior.
  • Use device and technology filters (iPhone users, high-end Android models, 4G/5G users) as purchasing power proxies.
  • Add qualification through lead form questions or landing page pre-qualification (travel timeframe, group size, budget range).
  • Expect CPM to increase, but overall lead quality, conversion rate, and cost per purchase should improve as intent and purchasing power rise.
